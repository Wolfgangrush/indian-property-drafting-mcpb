# State Exemplar — West Bengal (Property)

## Applicable Stamp Act
- **Act:** Indian Stamp Act 1899 as in force in West Bengal, with WB-specific amendments

## Article-wise stamp duty (verify current notification)

| Instrument | Article | Rate (typical) |
|---|---|---|
| Gift Deed (blood-relative) | 33 | ₹1,000 - ₹5,000 nominal (concessional) |
| Gift Deed (non-relative) | 33 | Same as Sale (typically 6%) |
| Exchange Deed | 31 | Higher leg at Sale rate; dual stamp |
| Release Deed (blood-relative) | 54 | Nominal |
| Release Deed (non-relative) | 54 | Same as Sale |
| Private Trust Deed | 63 | Linked to corpus value |
| Wakf Deed | 64 | Concessional |
| Easement Deed | 5 | Nominal |
| Partition Deed | 45 (Section 7 ISA) | Concessional among coparceners |
| Family Settlement Deed | 57 | Nominal |
| Mortgage Deed (registered) | 39 | Ad valorem on secured amount |
| Equitable Mortgage Memorandum | 6 | Lower rate |

## Market Value (WB Ready Reckoner equivalent)
- **Authority:** Directorate of Registration and Stamp Revenue, West Bengal
- **Portal:** https://wbregistration.gov.in
- **Application:** Higher of consideration and Market Value is the ad valorem basis

## Sub-Registrar circles
- Kolkata — Sub-Registrar circles
- District headquarters + tehsil-level Sub-Registrar offices across the State

## Mutation / Revenue Records
- **Agricultural land:** RoR (Record of Rights) / khatian under WB Land Reforms Act 1955
- **Urban property:** Kolkata Municipal Corporation (KMC) records
- **Flats in CHS:** West Bengal Co-operative Societies Act 2006

## Dayabhaga school
- West Bengal historically follows the Dayabhaga school of Hindu Mitakshara law — different inheritance and coparcener framework
- Father is the absolute owner during his lifetime; coparcenership arises only on father's death
- Post-2005 Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act 2005 applies as in other States

## West Bengal-specific overlays
- **West Bengal Apartment Ownership Act 1972** — for flats
- **West Bengal Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Rules 2017** — RERA (initially WB had a separate WB-HIRA Act, struck down by Supreme Court in 2021; RERA central Act applies)
- **West Bengal Land Reforms Act 1955** — ceiling restrictions on agricultural land
